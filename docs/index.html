What does this mean?

  • Gage height shows how high the water surface is at a monitoring station.
  • Discharge measures how much water passes the station per second.
  • Rate of rise can show how quickly conditions are changing, but it is not a flood warning.
  • Detention basins are intended to hold excess stormwater and release it more slowly after peak runoff.
  • Channel improvements are intended to improve conveyance; evaluating effectiveness requires hydraulic analysis beyond this dashboard.

Dutch translation

During a Netherlands water-resilience program, visits to Kinderdijk, the Delta Works, TU Delft, Delfland Water Authority, and Living Lab Scheveningen emphasized a shared idea: infrastructure works best when water is monitored, normal use is preserved where possible, and intervention is timed to changing conditions. This dashboard translates that principle into a Houston context by making the observed response of Hunting Bayou and the purpose of local flood-control infrastructure easier to interpret.
